Chapter: Integrals (Step by Step)

1. What is an Integral?

An integral is the opposite of a derivative.

  • Derivative = slope / rate of change.

  • Integral = accumulation / total amount.

Think of an integral as adding up infinitely many small pieces.

Two main types:

  1. Indefinite integral: general antiderivative, with +C+C+C (constant of integration).
  2. Definite integral: area under the curve between two xxx values.

2. The Indefinite Integral

The indefinite integral finds the family of functions whose derivative is the given function.

∫f(x) dx=F(x)+C\int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + C∫f(x)dx=F(x)+C

  • ∫\int∫: the integral symbol (“sum up”).

  • f(x)f(x)f(x): the function to integrate (integrand).

  • dxdxdx: variable of integration (“with respect to $x$”).

  • F(x)F(x)F(x): a function such that F′(x)=f(x)F'(x) = f(x)F′(x)=f(x).

  • CCC: constant of integration (because derivatives lose constants).

Example

∫2x dx=x2+C\int 2x \, dx = x^2 + C∫2xdx=x2+C

3. The Definite Integral

A definite integral is the area under a curve between x=ax=ax=a and x=bx=bx=b:

∫abf(x) dx\int_a^b f(x)\, dx∫ab​f(x)dx

  • aaa: lower bound.

  • bbb: upper bound.

  • The result is a number (area).

4. The Limit Definition (Connection to Sums)

The definite integral is defined as a limit of sums:

∫abf(x) dx=lim⁡n→∞∑i=1nf(xi) Δx\int_a^b f(x)\, dx = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i) \, \Delta x∫ab​f(x)dx=n→∞lim​i=1∑n​f(xi​)Δx

  • Break the interval [a,b][a,b][a,b] into nnn slices.

  • Δx\Delta xΔx: width of each slice.

  • f(xi)f(x_i)f(xi​): height at a sample point.

  • Multiply width ×\times× height = rectangle area.

  • Add them up, then let slices get infinitely thin.

This is called a Riemann sum.
